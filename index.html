Introduction to MTDSR2015 database

MTDSR2015 is the first public and free mandarin database for text-dependent speaker verification recorded by smartphones. The database is free to academic users but no permition for commercial applications. MTDSR2015 database aims to provide the community with sufficient mandarin corpus for prompted text speaker verification research for smart phone applications. It currently contains 52940 audio recording from 181 speakers including 102 male speakers and 79 female speakers. Each speaker was recorded with five parts:

     1. Twenty 8-digit sequences
     2. Fifteen poems
     3. Fifteen news sentences
     4. Twenty to thirty phrases and daily expressions unequally
     5. Two lyrics

It is known that channel mismatch problem severely affects the performance of all voiceprint based identity authentication systems, especially for smartphones. Therefore, in MTDSR2015, we take this problem into account. Considering the popularity in China market, four top selling smartphone models are selected as voice recorders including iPhone 5C, Samsung Note3 HUAWEI mate7 and XM4.

For MTDSR2015 database, we consider the demography of the population in terms of age and region which are often considered as two main criteria that affect the speaker verification engines. Speakers aged from 22 to 51 are selected, which is consistent with the age range of the target population who use smartphones more often. Additionally, considering the effect of the regions of the speakers, all the target 28 provinces and areas in China where people mainly speak mandarin have been covered.

MTDSR2015 was collected in uncontrolled laboratory and office environments. All speeches are recorded without adding additional noise. And the speeches for each speaker were recorded by four different smartphones simultaneously.

CONTENT

The entire package involves the full set of speech and language resources required to establish a Chinese speech recognition system.
